Branding Project

/ˈbran-diŋ prä,jekt/

Time: Winter 2020

Project Type: Academic

Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator, Indesign

The branding project is a series of projects in my GBDA101 course. In this course, we were assigned "clients" (who were classmates in the course) to design a brand for. These projects ranged from a poster design, a design pitch book, and an animation. It was not required for our projects to have cohesion, but I liked the direction the brand was initially going after the first project, so I decided to continue with it.

The Poster

The first part of this project was to design a poster and I decided to create the poster by using both Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator. My "client" said her design aesthetic is a blend between a vintage look and an Earth-toned color palette. Her main hobby was also dancing.

During my initial sketching phase, I thought that her design aesthetic and her hobby would be very difficult to represent and ran into multiple roadblocks. So, I looked at many posters on Pinterest and took notes on how they used design elements or symbols to represent different things. I soon came up with two ideas, to use the Earth-toned color palette accent a movement-based silhouette. Once I was set in this direction, the posters looked too plain for my liking. It also lacked the feeling of dance and movement that I was going for, so I added hands poised and contorted in various poses to accentuate this meaning.

After showing this to my Professor, he said that it would look great in a series of different posters and that it had potential as a marketing campaign. Taking his criticism into consideration, I strayed away from my initial color palette and leaned towards colors that resembled dancing, but were still easy on the eyes.

The Logo

After the poster, we were assigned to create a logo for our client, but before we could start, we had to submit a mood board. I decided to style the mood board based on three main takeaways that I got from my client during my interview with her:

I included one of the posters that I created because I would be referencing them for inspiration during the sketching phase of the logo. Most of the pictures that I put on the mood board were taken from Pinterest and were selected because they would make the mood board diverse and unique.

Once I finished the mood board, I started to sketch out designs and ideas for how I would want the logo to look. At first, I was playing around with different font styles with my client's initials to see if I could get any more ideas. However, I didn't like any of the ideas I was thinking of and started to reference the mood board more intently.

After a bit of brainstorming, I started to settle on an idea that I believed would look good if well executed. The idea was loosely based on the rings of a tree trunk, and the rings would be shaped into the client's initials. I liked the idea on paper, so I went onto Adobe Illustrator and started to make mockups. Unfortunately, I didn't like how it was beginning to turn out so I scrapped it and went back to sketching.

I started to experiment with different fonts again on Adobe Illustrator and found a font that flows into itself very nicely. I experimented more and created a very fluid logo with my client's initials.

After creating the logo, I needed to create multiple mockups of the logo on various products. I also needed to include the logo, the mockups, and an overall briefing of the design in a design pitch.

Once I showed my Professor my progress, he told me to play around with the logo some more. So, I went back into Adobe Illustrator and played around with different patterns that the logo could create. In the end, I created two variant logos, one for a "Lifestyle" aspect of the brand, and another for "Luxury".

The Animation

I didn't have much experience animating, but I had some knowledge on how to manipulate shapes and forms to create a motion. Watching cartoons and anime also inspired me on different effects to play around with. So I wanted to create a simple yet dynamic ball animation for the dancing brand that I created for my client.

I showed an early storyboard to my professor and he told me to add music or some sort of background noise. I had no experience in producing or creating any music but I thought that I would give it a shot. I ended up using a free online website to create a hip-hop beat that would become the basis of the animation.

Self-critique and Improvements

I did not learn that much from this project as I would have liked to, in fact, I used this project as a benchmark to apply the skills that I learned from high school. This will be used as a reference project to see how far I have gotten in my design journey since it's the first project where I put a considerable amount of passion into it.

I think that learning the background of my "client" really helped with the basic structure of this project as I was able to have an outline of what I wanted things to look like. Contrasting to other projects, I have had no consideration of how certain design elements would impact the overall feeling of the design. Moving forward, I will be applying this much more frequently since it made the final product look more cohesive.
