Royaltymine

/ˈroiəltēmīn/

Time: Summer 2021

Project Type: Work

Tools: Adobe Creative Suite, Figma

Royaltymine is a platform where artists around the world fund and monetize every aspect of their career through a global audience. As the sole designer within the company as an intern, I was tasked with rebuilding the brand identity from scratch while designing marketing promotions and redesigning the company website.

Preview the Project on Figma

The initial website had bright pinks and purples, with a gradient theme. It was really harsh to look at, and difficult to use since there was no hierarchy of features. I worked closely with the founder during my internship since he wanted to get the branding right, and I wanted to visualize his ideas.

Design System

I created a variety of colour palettes to see what direction the founder wished to go towards, however we ended up choosing a colour palette that has the same pink as the original brand, but with a lot of white for visual pacing.

The founder wanted to have illustrations throughout the website, so I created a small mood board for him to get an idea of what he was looking for. I ended up creating a design language for the illustrations that matches the mood board later on.

With the colours and the visual elements arranged, I created the design system which I would reference frequently when creating prototypes. It provided the consistency needed for the new website, since the original version lacked a strong enough brand identity.

First Iteration

The first iteration I designed had placeholder images instead of the planned illustrations. I wanted to make a more modernized landing page layout where the user is provided with information about Royaltymine. The original website displayed various songs on their landing page so I incorporated that into the design.

The colouring scheme I settled on reminded me of highlighters so a lot of the components of this initial design revolved around "highlighting" specific things. The new white background is more easily digestible to user's in contrast to the original harsh pink and purple background.

Designing Illustrations

We went through many different sketches and ideas when designing the illustrations for the website. By having these sketches, I was able to get helpful feedback, and it helped me explore a few options I would not have thought of if I didn't have them.

I decided to have the main visual elements be shapes representing people. Since I wasn't confident in my ability to design more realistic human proportions, geometric shapes were a great alternative as I could manipulate the expressions and poses to a great extent.

After repeating this process multiple times, we ended up with three illustrations to work with for the landing page. These illustrations would visualize a story that Royaltymine would tell.
